## Layer by Layer

### Layer 1: Identity (World ID)

**Problem:** Existing identity requires government permission.

**Solution:** World ID proves you're human without revealing who you are.

- Zero-knowledge proof of personhood
- One person = one credential
- No name, no documents, no government
- Sybil-resistant — can't create fake people

**For parallel society:** You exist in the new system without asking the old system for permission.

---

### Layer 2: Awareness (Governance Agent)

**Problem:** You can't parallel a system you can't see.

**Solution:** AI agent that discovers exactly who/what governs you.

- 33 officials, 12,847 laws — your governance stack
- Decision context — "before you do X, here's the governance"
- Local opportunities — businesses, initiatives near you
- Change monitoring — elections, new laws, redistricting

**For parallel society:** Know what you're building alternatives to. Find others under same governance. Coordinate.

---

### Layer 3: Economy (TIME Token)

**Problem:** Banks control money. "Investment" extracts from communities.

**Solution:** TIME — a currency you earn by existing and contributing.

- Birthright: 1 TIME per day you've lived (UBI)
- Work: 1 TIME per hour contributed
- Trade: Swap for BTC, ETH, USD, anything
- Local: Allocate to people/businesses you know

**For parallel society:** Economic activity without banks. Value flows locally. Community strengthens.

---

### Layer 4: Governance (Staking + Voting)

**Problem:** Political voice is separate from economic participation.

**Solution:** Stake TIME → gain voting power. Economy IS governance.

- Stake TIME to gain votes
- Quadratic voting (√staked = votes) prevents plutocracy
- Vote on protocol parameters, community decisions
- Economic skin in game = political voice

**For parallel society:** Those who participate in the economy govern it. No separate political class.

---

### Layer 5: Coordination (Federation)

**Problem:** Parallel communities are isolated, can't coordinate.

**Solution:** Shared infrastructure enables federation.

- Find others under same governance jurisdiction
- Form local parallel structures
- Federate across communities for larger decisions
- Shared standards, interoperable systems

**For parallel society:** Not one global alternative — many local alternatives that can work together.

## How It Works Together

### Example: Building a Local Food Co-op

**Layer 1 (Identity)**

- One person = one membership

**Layer 2 (Awareness)**

- Finds other food initiatives in area
- Discovers potential suppliers in network

**Layer 3 (Economy)**
- Members contribute TIME to launch
- Co-op accepts TIME for purchases
- Suppliers paid in TIME
- Workers earn TIME + Work NFTs

**Layer 4 (Governance)**
- Members stake TIME for voting power
- Vote on product selection, hours, policies
- Economic stake = governance voice

**Layer 5 (Coordination)**
- Connect with co-ops in neighboring areas
- Bulk purchasing federation
- Shared supply chain coordination

**Result:** A food co-op that:
- Didn't need government permission to organize
- Understood the governance landscape before starting
- Operates in parallel currency
- Is governed by its participants
- Can coordinate with others

**This is parallel society in action.**
